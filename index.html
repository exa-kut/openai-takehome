The Process

At MIT Sloan, Professor So set out to create a detailed case study on corporate earnings management. While a project like this typically demands hours of research, sourcing, and synthesis, using Deep Research, Professor So was able to generate a structured, 16-page draft in minutes.

With a single, detailed prompt, Professor So asked for a case study that blended historical analysis, financial data, regulatory context, and narrative storytelling. Before producing the draft, Deep Research asked a series of clarifying questions that helped align the output with his goals. Six minutes later, it delivered a 16-page draft complete with citations from NYU, the SEC, Reuters, and more.

Professor So reviewed and refined the draft by checking sources, customizing examples, and adding discussion prompts and visual exhibits. Deep Research provided a working draft that Professor So could evaluate and shape, freeing him from the early legwork of sourcing material and structuring the narrative.

This approach allowed him to focus on pedagogical value rather than starting from scratch. By using Deep Research to surface relevant themes and source material, Professor So could concentrate on tailoring the case to his students—shaping the structure, tone, and learning goals with intention. His process illustrates how expert users can partner with tools like Deep Research to speed up complex workflows without sacrificing quality or depth.

How Professor So Worked with Deep Research

This breaks down Professor So's workflow at MIT so you can apply the same approach to your own research projects. Each step includes what happened in his case and what you should consider for yours.

1

DEFINING RESEARCH INTENT

Professor So at MIT Sloan wanted to draft a comprehensive case study on "Expectations Management and the Earnings Game"—aiming to accelerate the research process without compromising academic depth.

→ What's the specific research question or topic you want to explore? Be as precise as possible about your end goal.
2

DRAFTING AN AMBITIOUS PROMPT

Professor So's initial request called for a story-driven introduction, historical and contemporary examples, regulatory framing, financial context, and APA-style citations.

→ Don't hold back—ask for everything you want. Deep Research works best with comprehensive, detailed prompts that specify format, tone, sources, and structure.
3

ENGAGING IN CLARIFYING DIALOGUE

Before generating output, Deep Research asked Professor So follow-up questions about industry focus, company selection, timeframe, and formatting—acting as an intelligent research partner.

→ Expect questions! This dialogue phase is crucial—answer thoughtfully as it shapes the entire research direction and quality of output.
4

GENERATING A FULL DRAFT

Deep Research returned a 16-page case study in just six minutes, complete with regulatory references, historical cases, and citations from 22 credible sources.

→ Be patient during generation—the more comprehensive your request, the longer it takes. The wait is worth it for the depth and sourcing quality.
5

REFINING AND CONTEXTUALIZING

Professor So evaluated the AI-generated draft alongside manually developed case studies, noting where AI succeeded and where human input—like visual aids, discussion prompts, and pedagogical framing—was still essential.

→ Treat the output as a sophisticated first draft. Add your expertise, domain knowledge, and unique insights to make it truly valuable for your specific audience.

Breaking Down Professor So's Original Prompt

"Write a case study on Expectations Management and the Earnings Game. Begin with a compelling and vivid narrative that introduces the key conflict or dilemma, engaging the reader with a real-world scenario. The first few pages should tell a story that sets the stage for the analytical discussion. After this introduction, provide a structured analysis that includes historical background, key actors, incentives, relevant regulations, and the consequences of the case. Incorporate deep research, financial data, regulatory excerpts, and impact analysis. The case study should follow a logical structure with an engaging introduction (story-driven), followed by an in-depth technical and analytical exploration of the topic, using academic sources and relevant examples."

Review: Evaluating Deep Research Output Quality

Professor So's evaluation process shows what to check when reviewing AI-generated research. Use this checklist to ensure your Deep Research outputs meet your quality standards.

1

FACT-CHECKING CITATIONS

Although Deep Research included 22 sources, none were verified by the model. Professor So notes that checking each citation was a necessary step before using the draft in an academic setting.

→ Always verify direct quotes, regulatory references, and case law.
2

SELECTING THE RIGHT EXAMPLES

Deep Research chose examples to match the prompt—but not all were ideal for Professor So's teaching goals. He swapped some out to better suit the audience and narrative.

→ Tailor examples to your specific use case, audience, or lesson goal.
3

ADDING VISUAL AIDS & EXHIBITS

The AI-generated case study included strong text-based structure—but no visuals. Professor So added graphs, data tables, and timelines that helped students better engage with the material.

→ Think: what would make this clearer or more memorable in your context?
4

CRAFTING DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

Deep Research didn't include questions for classroom dialogue. Professor So wrote his own prompts to spark reflection and debate.

→ Use your expertise to turn raw material into learning experiences.
5

REFINING FOR VOICE & CLARITY

While the AI output was impressive, it wasn't always concise or pedagogically precise. Professor So edited for clarity, tone, and flow.

→ Consider whether the language aligns with your teaching or communication style.
